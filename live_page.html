Ishigaki Island

Must Visit

Kabira Bay

An emerald world recognized by the world—a must-visit when coming to Ishigaki.

Awarded three stars in the Michelin Green Guide Japon, Kabira Bay stands as Ishigaki’s most celebrated scenic spot.
The sea changes color with the tides, offering something new to discover with every visit.
Explore the ocean floor through glass-bottom boat tours, or soak in the sweeping panoramic views from the observation deck above.
📍View location on Google Map

💡 Travel Tips

  • ・Snack shops, souvenir stores, and clean restrooms are available nearby.
  • ・Free parking is located 50m past the paid parking area.
  • ・For that photo-perfect view you’ve seen all over social media, make your way to the Kabira Park Observation Deck. This angle perfectly captures the Kabira Blue gradation.
  • ・While the main beach is busy with glass-bottom boats, walking north along the shoreline leads to a much quieter area. It feels like a private beach—highly recommended for a tranquil moment.

Snorkeling

Yonehara Beach

Ishigaki's premier tropical fish paradise. Experience this beauty for yourself.

The transparent shallow waters of Yonehara

Quiet morning, crystal green sea. The overwhelming vitality of Yonehara, discovered through a real run.

Yonehara Beach is truly a "natural aquarium." With its flourishing coral reefs, you'll find an incredible number of tropical fish swimming even in knee-deep shallows.
Running along the shoreline, the crunch of coral underfoot and the salt-tinged breeze through the trees awaken your senses.
In an island where development continues, a place with such rich, untouched nature remains a precious treasure.
📍View location on Google Map

💡 Travel Tips

  • ・🚿 Showers, restrooms, and shop facilities are fully equipped, making it perfect for post-swim care.
  • ・⚠️ Caution for rip currents: While beautiful, strong currents can occur in certain areas. Always wear a life jacket and stay aware of your surroundings.

Taketomi Island

Just 10 minutes by boat from Ishigaki. Old Okinawa, preserved.

Red-tiled villages and coral sand paths. In Taketomi Island, there is “authentic Okinawa” that has been strictly protected.
Lunch hours can be irregular on the island, so my recommendation is to buy a bento at the Ishigaki Port terminal and enjoy it on the beach.

Best Beach

Kondoi Beach

One of Yaeyama's finest shallow-water beaches. At low tide, a 'phantom sandbar' emerges in the middle of the sea.

A spacious, pure white sand beach under the tropical sun.

The emerald green water is enhanced by the perfectly clear, sandy seabed.

Taketomi’s premier beach, where crystal-clear tropical waters stretch as far as the eye can see.
The seabed exists in its purest form, with absolutely no rocks or corals. Free of any impurities, the water stays impossibly clear, glowing with a vivid emerald green. It is, quite literally, a natural swimming pool.
My top recommendation is to walk the 500-meter shoreline from Kondoi Beach to Kaiji Beach. A stroll along the water’s edge here is a luxury unique to this island.
📍View location on Google Map

💡 Travel Tips

  • ・Securing Beverages: No vending machines are available on the beach. Be sure to purchase plenty of drinks at the Ishigaki Port terminal or in Taketomi village before heading out.
  • ・Sun Protection: There is absolutely no shade, and no parasol rentals are available. Hats, rash guards, and sunscreen are non-negotiable essentials.
  • ・Basic toilets and showers are available near the entrance.

Heritage Village

Taketomi Village

A red-tiled village where time stands still. A preservation district of unique Ryukyuan heritage.

Beautiful traditional streetscape with coral stone walls and red-tile roofs.

Detours are the best part of walking this island.

A designated preservation district located at the heart of the island.
While visiting a popular cafe is a great plan, I suggest turning down a random alleyway to truly feel the island's soul.
Vibrant hibiscus blooming over stone walls, the faint tracks of water buffalo carts in the white sand, and the deep, peaceful silence. Immerse yourself in the extraordinary flow of time unique to this island.

Travel Guide

✈️ Getting There

Flights to Painushima Ishigaki Airport (New Ishigaki Airport)

Naha (approx. 1h), Tokyo (approx. 3h), Osaka (approx. 2.5h)※Operated by ANA, JAL, Solaseed Air, and Peach

☀️ Best Season

April – October
This is the best period for enjoying the ocean. Specifically, late June right after the rainy season ends offers stable sunny weather and crystal-clear waters.

🌙 Duration

3 Nights, 4 Days+
Allow 2 days for Ishigaki itself, plus at least 1 day for Taketomi or other islands. A relaxed schedule is recommended to fully experience the charm of Yaeyama.

💰 Budget

60,000–120,000 JPY /person (3N4D)
※Estimated cost including round-trip airfare, accommodation, rental car, and meals.

🚗 Transportation

A rental car is essential for getting around Ishigaki.

⭐ Early Rental Car Booking

Since availability is limited, it’s a rule to secure your rental car as soon as you book your flights.

🚌 Airport Bus

Travel from the airport to the Ferry Terminal takes approx. 30–40 min (approx. 550 JPY). Direct buses (Karry Kanko) and local lines (Azuma Bus) operate frequent services.

⛴️ Inter-island Ferry

High-speed ferries depart from the terminal to Taketomi (approx. 10–15 min), Iriomote (approx. 40–50 min), and more. Aney Kanko and Yaeyama Kanko Ferry cover the major routes.

⚠️ Important Notes

  • 🚌
    Buses between the airport and the Ferry Terminal do not accept IC cards (Suica, PASMO, etc.). Prepare cash or use touch-payment credit cards (on selected routes).
  • 🚗
    It takes about 30–40 minutes to drive from the city center to Kabira Bay. Ishigaki is surprisingly large, so allow plenty of time for travel.
  • 🍕
    Restaurants on Taketomi often have short hours or irregular holidays. A smart move is to secure food at the Ferry Terminal before crossing to the island.
  • 🅿️
    For Kabira Bay parking, there is a free public parking lot just past the paid one. I recommend checking the back area first.
  • 🤿
    Yonehara Beach is great for snorkeling, but rip currents can occur. Always check lifeguard instructions and signs, and ensure you wear a life jacket.

FAQ

Q

How do I get to Taketomi Island from Ishigaki Island?

A
It takes about 10–15 minutes by high-speed ferry from Ishigaki Port Ferry Terminal. There are frequent departures, making it ideal for a day trip.
Q

Do I need a rental car for sightseeing in Ishigaki Island?

A
Yes, a rental car is essential for efficient travel as spots are spread across the large island. On the other hand, rental bicycles or walking are common for exploring Taketomi Island.
Q

When is the best season to visit Ishigaki and Taketomi?

A
April to October is best for enjoying the ocean. The period after the rainy season ends in late June is particularly beautiful. For walking around Taketomi Village, the cooler autumn months are also recommended.
Q

Are there recommended running routes or times?

A
We recommend coastal routes or the area around the Southern Gate Bridge for early morning runs. Since the midday sun is intense, morning or sunset runs are best. Exploring the backstreets is also a uniquely island experience.
Q

How do I get from the airport to the city center (Ferry Terminal)?

A
It takes about 30–40 minutes by bus from the airport to the Ferry Terminal (approx. 550 JPY). There are direct buses (Karry Kanko) and local lines (Azuma Bus). As IC cards are generally not accepted, we recommend preparing cash.
Q

How do I access Ishigaki Island (Painushima Ishigaki Airport)?

A
It takes about 1 hour from Naha Airport, 3 hours from Tokyo (Haneda/Narita), and 2.5 hours from Osaka (Itami/Kansai). Airlines like ANA, JAL, Solaseed Air, and Peach operate flights.
Q

What is the estimated budget for traveling to Ishigaki and Taketomi?

A
For a 3-night, 4-day trip, expect to spend about 60,000 to 120,000 JPY per person. This includes round-trip airfare, accommodation, rental car, and meals. Prices fluctuate during high season and holidays, so early booking is recommended.
Q

Do I need to book ferries (high-speed boats) in advance?

A
For high-speed ferries to nearby islands like Taketomi, you can usually buy tickets on the day of travel. However, we recommend online booking during peak seasons (Golden Week, summer holidays) to avoid long queues and ensure a smooth boarding process.
